Udacity Professional Profile

1. Introduction

What are Profiles?

A Udacity Professional Profile is your direct connection to Udacity's hiring partners. Using Talent Source, a directory of Udacity Profiles, recruiters search for candidates like yourself to fill a plethora of positions ranging from short-term, contract opportunities to full-time jobs.

How Can I Get Into Talent Source?

Once you have completed two Nanodegree projects, you can enable your Profile's "recruiter access." Then, employers will be able to see your Profile if you show up in their search results. In this course, we'll go over how to optimize your Profile for search to attract the right employers. Before we get there, let's talk about how Talent Source works.

2. Udacity Talent Source

How Talent Source Works for Employers

Once signed-in to Talent Source, employers can search through students' and graduates' Profiles using search terms such as position (ie: Android Developer, Machine Learning Engineer), location, skills, and work type (ie: remote, part-time, full-time). A filtered list of candidates, as shown below, pulls up. A recruiter can browse through your Profile and be linked to your GitHub or LinkedIn profiles among others, download your resume or projects, and view your work history. They will be able to email you directly with opportunities. Check out the employer view of Talent Source:

In addition, when a hiring partner asks Udacity to recommend job candidates, the Udacity Careers Team will review Profiles through Talent Source to find matches.

Your Professional Profile will be most powerful after your other career materials are ready, so make sure your resume, LinkedIn, GitHub and/or Kaggle, and other portfolio items are up to date.
